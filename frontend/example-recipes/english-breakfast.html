English Breakfast

Also known as a "Fry Up," a traditional English breakfast varies by household and region, but the heart of it stays the same: sausages, bacon, eggs, baked beans, tomatoes, mushrooms, and bread — all cooked in the same pan, one after another, layered with flavor. You probably will not be hungry again until dinner.

This recipe is written for one serving. Multiply all amounts by the number of people you are feeding.

Recipe in 6 parts:

  • Part 1: Prepare your workspace and ingredients
  • Part 2: Cook the sausages and bacon
  • Part 3: Toast the bread
  • Part 4: Cook the mushrooms and tomatoes
  • Part 5: Warm the baked beans
  • Part 6: Fry the eggs and plate

Estimated time: About one and a half to two hours

Ingredients

  • 2 breakfast sausage links
    • Cumberland sausages are traditional — pork links seasoned with mace, sage, nutmeg, and pepper. They run slightly larger than American breakfast links, so adjust the number based on appetite.
  • 4 ounces of bacon, ideally a thick slab cut rather than thin strips — pork back bacon, which comes from the loin, is traditional
  • 1 or 2 eggs
    • Eggs can be poached, scrambled, or fried. Fried is traditional, and that is how they are prepared here.
  • 2 ounces of mushrooms, quartered
    • Baby Bella mushrooms are traditional, but white button mushrooms work well too.
  • 1 Roma or plum tomato, halved lengthwise — the oval shape holds together better during cooking
  • 1 medium-thick slice of French bread
  • 1 can of baked beans in tomato sauce — Heinz is the traditional choice
  • 4 teaspoons of butter, divided across the steps
  • 1 tablespoon of neutral vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce (optional, for the beans)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Part 1: Prepare Your Workspace and Ingredients

Step 1

Preheat your oven to 170 degrees Fahrenheit. This will serve as a warming drawer as each component finishes cooking.

Step 2

Line a small baking sheet with parchment paper and place it near the oven. This is where cooked items will rest and stay warm as you work through the recipe.

Step 3

Rinse the tomato and mushrooms under cool running water and pat them dry. Quarter the mushrooms and halve the tomato lengthwise. Place them in separate small bowls on your work surface so you can reach them easily when needed.

Recipe Tip: Keeping each prepped ingredient in its own bowl before cooking means you will always know exactly where each item is and can reach for it without searching.

TBK Tools:

  1. Cut Glove — wear on your non-dominant hand while quartering the mushrooms and halving the tomato to protect your fingers from the knife.
  2. Tool-free: Curl your fingertips inward and use your knuckles to guide the blade, keeping fingertips well back from the cutting edge.

Step 4

Set out your raw sausages and bacon on a plate or tray near the stove. Place your tongs, a fork, and a talking thermometer close by.

TBK Tools:

  1. Work Trays — use a tray with raised edges to corral the raw sausages, bacon, and utensils in one organised spot near the stove so nothing rolls or shifts out of reach.
  2. Tool-free: Use a rimmed baking sheet or a large shallow bowl to keep all the items grouped together in a known location.

Step 5

Open the can of baked beans and set it near the stove with a small saucepan.

Part 2: Cook the Sausages and Bacon

Step 6

Add one tablespoon of neutral oil to a frying pan large enough to hold the sausages and bacon in a single layer without crowding. Warm the pan over low heat. When the oil is warm — you will feel gentle heat rising if you hold your hand a few inches above the pan — add the two sausage links. You should hear only a very faint, quiet sizzle. If the sizzle is loud or spitting, the heat is too high.

TBK Tools:

  1. Auto-Measure Spout — fits onto the oil bottle and dispenses exactly one tablespoon per pour, so you do not need to measure separately.
  2. Tool-free: Pre-measure one tablespoon of oil into a small bowl before you begin, then pour it into the pan from the bowl.

Step 7

Cook the sausages over low heat, turning them occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 160 degrees Fahrenheit when tested with your talking thermometer. This will take roughly 12 to 15 minutes depending on thickness.

Recipe Tip: Insert the thermometer probe into the thickest part of the sausage through one end, not through the side, to get the most accurate reading and avoid piercing through to the pan.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Thermometer — announces the internal temperature of the sausage aloud so you can confirm doneness without reading a display.
  2. Tool-free: Use a timer set for 15 minutes over low heat as a reliable guide — sausages of standard thickness cooked low and slow will be safely cooked through by that point.

Step 8

Once the sausages are cooked through, add the four ounces of bacon to the same pan. Cook for 2 minutes, then flip the bacon and cook for another 2 minutes. The bacon should feel firm when pressed gently with the back of your tongs — it will have lost its soft, floppy give and will feel noticeably stiffer and more resistant when pressed.

TBK Tools:

  1. Long Wood Tongs — keep your hands well away from the hot fat when turning the bacon and sausages in the pan.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled tongs or a fork with a long handle to turn the bacon, keeping your wrist and forearm clear of the pan.

Step 9

Using tongs, transfer the sausages and bacon to the parchment-lined baking sheet and place it in the warm oven to rest while you cook the remaining components.

TBK Tools:

  1. Oven Rack Guards — protect your forearms from contact with the oven rack when sliding the baking sheet in or pulling it out.
  2. Tool-free: Use a folded kitchen towel draped over the oven rack as a barrier when reaching in, and pull the rack out fully before placing or retrieving the baking sheet.

Part 3: Toast the Bread

Step 10

Add one teaspoon of butter to the same frying pan over medium-low heat. When the butter has melted and you can smell it beginning to foam — a warm, slightly nutty scent — place the one slice of French bread flat in the pan. Press it gently with the back of a spatula so it makes full contact with the pan.

TBK Tools:

  1. Dry Measure Spoons — use to measure each teaspoon of butter accurately throughout the recipe.
  2. Tool-free: Use a standard teaspoon from your cutlery drawer, levelling off the butter with your finger to gauge an approximate teaspoon.

Step 11

After about 1 to 2 minutes, when the bread feels firm and resistant when you press it lightly (rather than soft and yielding), flip it and toast the second side for another 1 to 2 minutes. Transfer the bread to the baking sheet in the oven to keep warm.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Countdown Timer — set it for 2 minutes per side so you know exactly when to flip the bread and when to transfer it, without watching a display.
  2. Tool-free: Count the time using any kitchen timer or a phone timer with an audible alert, set for 2 minutes per side.

Part 4: Cook the Mushrooms and Tomatoes

Step 12

Add one teaspoon of butter to the pan over medium heat. Add the two ounces of quartered mushrooms. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms release their moisture and become soft and fragrant — this takes about 3 to 4 minutes. You will notice the earthy, savory smell intensify as they cook, and they will feel soft and yielding when pressed with a spoon rather than firm and springy. Transfer them to the oven to keep warm.

TBK Tools:

  1. Long Wood Spoon — keeps your hand safely away from the hot pan while stirring the mushrooms and later the beans.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled spoon to stir, keeping your forearm clear of the pan and steam.

Step 13

Add another teaspoon of butter to the pan. Place the halved tomato cut-side down in the pan. Cook for about 2 minutes — you should hear a gentle sizzle and smell the tomato beginning to soften and sweeten. Flip carefully using a spatula or tongs and cook for another 2 minutes. The tomato should feel soft when gently pressed but should still hold together and offer light resistance rather than collapsing under your touch. Transfer to the oven.

Recipe Tip: Keeping the tomato cut-side down first helps it stay intact. The skin side is sturdier and will support the softened flesh when you flip it.

TBK Tools:

  1. Double Spatula — the two-pronged design stabilises the soft tomato halves when flipping them, reducing the risk of them breaking apart or sliding unexpectedly.
  2. Tool-free: Slide a wide, flat spatula fully under the tomato before lifting, and use a second spoon or fork on top to steady it as you turn it over.

Part 5: Warm the Baked Beans

Pour the can of baked beans into a small saucepan. Add one teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce and one teaspoon of butter if using. Set over the lowest possible heat and stir occasionally. The beans are already cooked — you are just warming them through. This will take about 5 to 8 minutes.

Recipe Tip: The Worcestershire sauce and butter are optional, but they add a savory depth that the beans lack straight from the can.

TBK Tools:

  1. Wet Measure Spoons — use to measure the teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce accurately without overpowering the beans.
  2. Tool-free: Pour a small amount of Worcestershire sauce into the cap of the bottle first, then tip it into the pan — most bottle caps hold roughly a teaspoon.

Part 6: Fry the Eggs and Plate

Step 15

Add the remaining one teaspoon of butter to the frying pan over low heat. When the butter has melted and you hear the faintest sizzle, crack one or two eggs into the pan. If you are using an egg ring, place it in the pan before adding the egg to help contain the white in a defined, contained area you can feel the edges of with a spoon or spatula.

TBK Tools:

  1. Egg Rings — placed in the pan before cracking the egg, they contain the white in a defined area so you always know exactly where the egg is sitting in the pan.
  2. Tool-free: Crack the egg into a small bowl first, then slide it gently into the centre of the pan from close range so the white settles in a compact, gathered mass that stays close together rather than spreading thinly across the pan.

Step 16

Cook the eggs over low heat without flipping. After about 2 minutes, carefully spoon a small amount of the hot butter from the edge of the pan over the top of the yolk. You will hear a soft sizzle as the butter contacts the egg. Repeat once or twice. The white should feel set and firm when gently touched at the edge — no longer wet or yielding under your fingertip, with no slippery or jelly-like give — while the yolk remains soft and gently domed, springing back slightly when barely touched rather than feeling flat or firm.

Recipe Tip: Basting the yolk with hot butter from the pan, rather than flipping the egg, cooks the top of the yolk gently without breaking it. This is the traditional method for a fried egg served sunny-side up.

TBK Tools:

  1. Tasting Spoon — its long handle keeps your hand and wrist well away from the hot pan when spooning butter over the yolk.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled spoon to baste, tilting the pan slightly toward you so the butter pools at the edge and is easier to scoop up.

Step 17

To plate, start with the slice of toasted bread as your base. Working around the plate in a consistent arrangement you can remember, place the sausages along one edge, the bacon beside them, and the tomato and mushrooms in the remaining space. Set the egg on top of or beside the toast. Spoon the warm beans into any remaining space on the plate, or serve them in a small bowl on the side to keep them from running into the other components.

Recipe Tip: Serving the beans in a small ramekin beside the plate gives you more control over the arrangement and keeps the plate tidy.

Enjoy!
