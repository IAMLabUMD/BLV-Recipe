Green Bean Casserole

Green Bean Casserole is a classic American side dish, especially popular at Thanksgiving and holiday gatherings. It comes together quickly once your ingredients are assembled, and the result is a warm, creamy, savory dish topped with crunchy, brittle fried onions.

This recipe is broken into 3 parts:

  • Part 1: Prepare and combine the ingredients
  • Part 2: Bake the casserole
  • Part 3: Add the topping and finish under the broiler

Estimated time: About 1 hour

Servings: Makes approximately 6 to 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 8 cups cooked green beans (two fourteen-and-a-half-ounce cans, drained, work well here)
  • 2 cans (21 ounces each) cream of mushroom condensed soup
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • a quarter teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 and two thirds cups canned French fried onions, divided into 2 equal portions

Ingredient Note: Cream of mushroom soup may be regular or reduced sodium. You can also substitute another cream soup, such as cream of onion or cream of potato, if preferred.

Instructions

Part 1: Prepare and Combine the Ingredients

Step 1

Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.

Recipe Tip: If your oven dial is not already marked with tactile indicators, a bump dot or small piece of tactile paint at the 350-degree position makes this easy to find consistently.

Step 2

Lightly grease a 3-quart oven-safe casserole dish and set it aside on your work surface.

Step 3

If using canned green beans, open the two cans and drain them thoroughly. Set the drained beans aside in a bowl.

TBK Tools:

  1. Side Strainer — clips to the pot or bowl so you can drain canned beans safely with one hand, keeping the lid or your hand away from the liquid stream.
  2. Tool-free: Hold the can lid firmly against the opening with your thumb, tilt the can over the sink, and drain slowly — a folded dish towel wrapped around the can gives extra grip and protects your hand from the sharp lid edge.

Step 4

Measure out the 2 and two thirds cups of French fried onions and divide them into 2 equal portions of one and one third cups each. Place each portion in a separate small bowl and set both aside.

Recipe Tip: A talking kitchen scale makes dividing the onions into equal portions straightforward — simply weigh the full container, then divide the total weight in half.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Scale — announces the weight aloud so you can divide the onions into two equal portions without needing to read a display.
  2. Tool-free: Use a Dry Measure Cup to scoop the onions — fill it to the one and one third cup level by feel, using your fingertip to level the top, and repeat for the second portion.

Step 5

Open the two cans of cream of mushroom soup and add them to a large mixing bowl.

Step 6

Add the one cup of milk to the bowl and stir until the soup and milk are fully combined and no lumps can be felt while stirring with a uniform, consistent feel throughout.

TBK Tools:

  1. Wet Measure Cups — tactile markings let you confirm the one cup level by touch rather than sight.
  2. Tool-free: Use a standard measuring cup and fill it to the brim — a one-cup measure holds exactly one cup when filled to the top, so you can feel the liquid reach the rim with a fingertip.

Step 7

Add the two teaspoons of soy sauce and the quarter teaspoon of ground black pepper to the bowl and stir to combine.

TBK Tools:

  1. Dry Measure Spoons — tactile spoons let you measure the quarter teaspoon of pepper accurately by feel.
  2. Tool-free: Pre-measure the soy sauce and pepper into small prep bowls before you begin, using standard measuring spoons filled level and confirmed by feel.

Step 8

Add the drained green beans to the bowl and stir until they are uniformly coated, with no dry or uncoated beans felt when stirring in the soup mixture.

TBK Tools:

  1. Long Wood Spoon — gives you a secure reach into a deep mixing bowl and lets you feel when the beans are fully coated without your hands contacting the mixture.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled spoon and stir in wide, slow circles from the bottom of the bowl upward to bring the soup mixture up and over the beans evenly.

Step 9

Add one portion of the French fried onions to the bowl and stir gently to distribute them throughout the mixture.

Step 10

Transfer the entire mixture into the prepared casserole dish, spreading it into a layer of consistent depth, with no raised mounds or sunken areas felt when you run the back of a spoon across it. Use the back of a spoon or a spatula to level the surface so it feels flat and even under the spoon and press the mixture gently into the corners of the dish.

TBK Tools:

  1. Bowl Scraper — removes the mixture cleanly from the mixing bowl so nothing is left behind, and lets you feel the bowl surface to confirm it is fully emptied.
  2. Tool-free: Use a flexible rubber spatula and work around the inside of the bowl in sections, pressing the spatula against the sides and base to capture all of the mixture.

Part 2: Bake the Casserole

Step 11

Place the casserole dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25 minutes. The casserole is ready when you can hear it bubbling actively around the edges and it gives off a warm, savory, mushroom-forward aroma.

Recipe Tip: Place the casserole dish on a baking sheet before putting it in the oven. This makes it much easier and safer to remove — you can grip the baking sheet rather than reaching for the casserole dish itself with oven mitts.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Countdown Timer — announces the remaining time aloud so you know exactly when the 25 minutes are up without needing to check a display.
  2. Tool-free: Set the timer on your phone or a speaking clock app to 25 minutes and rely on the bubbling sound and savory aroma as additional cues that the casserole is ready.

Step 12

Using heat-resistant oven gloves, carefully remove the casserole from the oven and set it on a heatproof surface.

TBK Tools:

  1. Oven Push-Pull — lets you slide the rack out and push it back in without your hands passing over the hot rack edge, reducing the risk of contact burns.
  2. Tool-free: Use a thick folded dish towel or silicone oven mitts that cover your wrists, and grip the baking sheet from the sides rather than reaching over the rack.

Part 3: Add the Topping and Finish Under the Broiler

Step 13

Switch your oven setting to broil.

Step 14

Scatter the second portion of French fried onions in a layer of consistent coverage over the top of the casserole, using your fingertips to feel for bare patches and fill them in. Do not stir them in — they should sit on the surface so they can firm up and feel rigid and brittle, giving off a toasty, nutty aroma under the broiler.

Recipe Tip: To distribute the onions evenly without seeing the surface, use your fingertips to lightly feel where onions have landed and fill in any bare patches. Work gently so you do not press them into the mixture.

Step 15

Return the casserole to the oven under the broiler for 3 to 4 minutes. The onions are ready when you can hear them sizzling steadily and detect a toasty, slightly nutty aroma. Do not walk away during this step, as the onions can go from firm and fragrant with a toasted smell to sharp, acrid, and burnt-smelling quickly.

TBK Tools:

  1. Oven Rack Guards — provide a tactile barrier along the rack edges so you can slide the casserole in and out under the broiler without your forearms making contact with the hot rack.
  2. Tool-free: Use long silicone oven mitts that extend past the wrist, and approach the rack from directly in front rather than from the side to keep your arms clear of the rack edges.

Step 16

Using heat-resistant oven gloves, remove the casserole from the oven and place it on a heatproof surface. Allow it to cool for 10 minutes before serving.

Recipe Tip: To serve, use a large spoon and work from the edges of the dish toward the center, which helps keep the brittle, rigid onion topping intact on each portion as you scoop.

Optional Variations

  • Stir in a half cup of shredded cheese in place of the soy sauce for a richer, cheesier casserole.
  • Add a half cup of finely diced red bell pepper to the mixture in Step 8 for extra flavour and a slight sweetness.
  • Crumble 3 strips of cooked, brittle bacon that snaps cleanly when broken and stir them into the mixture in Step 8 for a smoky note.
