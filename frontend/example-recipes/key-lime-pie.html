Key Lime Meringue Pie

This classic pie has a bright, tangy filling made from fresh lime juice and sweetened condensed milk, topped with a light, airy and pillowy meringue that feels soft and springy when touched. The recipe is broken into three parts: making the filling, preparing the meringue, and assembling and baking the finished pie.

This recipe makes 8 servings and takes approximately 1 hour of active cooking time.

Recipe in 3 parts:

  • Part 1: Make the lime filling
  • Part 2: Make the meringue
  • Part 3: Assemble and bake

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 1 fully-baked 9-inch graham cracker or pastry pie shell
  • 1 fourteen-ounce can sweetened condensed milk
  • a half cup fresh Key lime juice
  • 4 egg yolks

For the Meringue:

  • 4 egg whites
  • a half cup caster sugar

Instructions

Part 1: Make the Lime Filling

Step 1

Separate the 4 eggs, placing the yolks in a large mixing bowl and the whites in a separate, completely clean and dry bowl. Set the whites aside — you will use them in Part 2.

Recipe Tip: Even a small trace of yolk in the egg whites will prevent the meringue from whipping properly. Crack each egg over a small cup first, then transfer the white to the larger bowl once you are sure there is no slippery, thicker yolk membrane present — the white should feel uniformly thin and watery with no dense or gel-like patch. An egg separator tool can make this easier and more reliable.

TBK Tools:

  1. Egg Separator — holds over the bowl and cleanly catches the yolk while letting the white fall through, reducing the risk of accidental yolk contamination.

Tool-free: Crack each egg into a small cup and use your fingers or a spoon to lift the yolk out, then transfer the confirmed white to the larger bowl.

Step 2

Add the one fourteen-ounce can of sweetened condensed milk to the bowl with the egg yolks and whisk until the mixture feels uniform when stirred, with no variation in resistance around the bowl.

Step 3

Add the a half cup of fresh Key lime juice and continue whisking until the mixture is free of any lumps or unevenness when felt while stirring and consistent in resistance throughout. The mixture will begin to resist the whisk slightly more, feeling thicker and less fluid as you stir as the acid in the lime juice reacts with the condensed milk.

Recipe Tip: If juicing limes yourself, strain the juice through a fine mesh strainer before measuring to remove any seeds and bitter pith. A talking scale or tactile measuring cup can help confirm you have the right amount.

TBK Tools:

  1. Wet Measure Cups — tactile markings let you confirm the half cup of lime juice by touch without needing to read a measuring cup visually.

Tool-free: Use a tablespoon measure and count out 8 level tablespoons, which equals a half cup.

Step 4

Pour the filling into the pre-baked pie shell. Use a spatula to scrape the bowl clean and spread the filling into an even layer by gently tilting the shell side to side and smoothing with the spatula until you feel no raised or sunken areas as the spatula passes across the surface.

TBK Tools:

  1. Bowl Scraper — removes filling cleanly and completely from the mixing bowl, helping you transfer every bit of filling into the shell without waste or mess.

Tool-free: Use a flexible silicone spatula and work around the bowl in sections, pressing firmly against the sides and base to collect all of the filling.

Step 5

Place the filled pie in the oven at 350 degrees Fahrenheit and bake for 8 minutes. The filling will still wobble and shift loosely in the center when you carefully nudge the pan — this is correct. Set the pie aside while you prepare the meringue.

Recipe Tip: Use oven rack guards and heat-resistant gloves when placing and removing the pie. Leave the oven on at 350 degrees Fahrenheit — you will need it again shortly.

TBK Tools:

  1. Oven Rack Guards — attach to the oven rack edges to create a tactile barrier that reduces the risk of burns when sliding the pie in or out.
  2. Heat Gloves — protect hands and forearms from oven heat when placing and retrieving the pie.
  3. Talking Countdown Timer — announces when the 8-minute bake time is complete so you do not need to watch a display.

Tool-free: Fit the guards to the rack before preheating if possible, use thick folded oven mitts in place of heat gloves, and set any audible timer you have available for 8 minutes.

Part 2: Make the Meringue

Step 6

Make sure the bowl you are using for the egg whites is completely clean, dry, and free of any grease. Any fat residue will prevent the whites from whipping to the right texture. Run your fingers around the inside of the bowl to check — it should feel smooth and dry with no slick or oily patches.

Step 7

Using a stand mixer with the whisk attachment, beat the 4 egg whites on high speed until soft peaks form — the mixture will feel significantly lighter and airier when you briefly dip a finger in. To check for soft peaks, stop the mixer and lift the whisk straight up out of the whites — the peak that forms should hold its shape for a moment under your fingertip before gently collapsing and folding over. This stage takes approximately 2 to 3 minutes.

Recipe Tip: The egg whites will also change audibly as they whip — at first they will sound wet and sloshing, then progressively quieter and thicker as they build volume and structure.

Step 8

With the mixer still running on high, add the a half cup of caster sugar gradually, one or two tablespoons at a time, waiting about 20 seconds between each addition. Continue beating until the meringue forms stiff peaks that hold firmly without collapsing, with a slightly sticky, smooth, and satiny feel.

Recipe Tip: To check for stiff peaks, stop the mixer and lift the whisk — the peak should stand straight up and hold completely rigid without folding over at all when you touch it lightly. The meringue will also feel significantly heavier and more resistant to the whisk than at the soft peak stage. Run a clean finger through the meringue — it should feel smooth, not gritty. If you can still feel sugar granules, continue beating for another minute before checking again.

TBK Tools:

  1. Dry Measure Spoons — let you measure out the caster sugar one or two tablespoons at a time by feel, giving you consistent additions without needing to estimate.

Tool-free: Pre-measure the full half cup of sugar into a small bowl before starting, then pinch and add small amounts gradually, pausing between each addition as described.

Part 3: Assemble and Bake

Step 9

Working quickly while the pie filling is still hot, spoon the meringue onto the center of the pie. Use the back of a spoon to spread it outward toward the edges, making sure the meringue makes full contact with the crust all the way around the rim. This seal prevents the meringue from pulling away from the edges and leaving a gap you can feel between the meringue and crust during baking.

Recipe Tip: Use one hand to lightly rest a finger on the edge of the pie crust to guide your position as you spread the meringue outward. Move slowly and deliberately from the center toward the edge you can feel.

Step 10

Once the meringue is spread with consistent resistance felt across the entire surface as the spoon passes over it, use the back of the spoon to create texture across the surface by pressing it gently into the meringue and pulling straight up to form small peaks that hold their shape and feel firm and springy when briefly touched. Repeat across the entire surface.

Step 11

Place the pie in the oven at 350 degrees Fahrenheit and bake for 10 to 15 minutes. The meringue is ready when it smells lightly toasted and nutty, and the surface feels dry and firm and set — not wet or sticky — when you carefully and very briefly touch the edge of the meringue with a fingertip.

Recipe Tip: The center of the meringue will finish slightly later than the edges. Focus your touch test on the outer portion first — if that feels dry and set, the center is likely close behind. If you prefer not to touch the meringue, you can use a visual interpretation service such as Be My Eyes to confirm doneness by its toasted, nutty aroma and the dry, firm feel of the surface.

TBK Tools:

  1. Oven Push-Pull — lets you slide the rack out and push it back in safely without leaning close to the oven opening, reducing burn risk when checking the meringue.

Tool-free: Use a long, folded oven mitt to grip the rack edge and pull it toward you before placing or retrieving the pie.

Step 12

Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool completely at room temperature before slicing, at least 1 hour. The filling will become noticeably firmer and more resistant when pressed lightly through the meringue as it cools.

Recipe Tip: A pie marker and divider tool can help you score and cut the pie into 8 equal portions without needing to estimate visually.

TBK Tools:

  1. Pie Marker and Divider — scores the meringue surface into 8 equal portions before cutting, so each slice is consistent in size, confirmed by equal resistance and spacing felt as the divider is pressed in without needing to estimate by eye.

Tool-free: Use a long knife and locate the center of the pie by touch, then make one cut straight across, rotate the pie a quarter turn, make another cut across, and then halve each quarter to produce 8 portions.
