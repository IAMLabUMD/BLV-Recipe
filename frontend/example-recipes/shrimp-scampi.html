Garlic Shrimp Scampi Pasta

Garlic Shrimp Scampi is a classic dish that comes together quickly — buttery, garlicky shrimp tossed with pasta and finished with a bright hit of lemon and a little heat from red chile flakes. Because this recipe moves fast once the cooking starts, having everything prepared and within reach before you turn on the stove will make the process much smoother.

Makes: 4 servings

Estimated time: about 45 minutes

Recipe in 4 parts:

  • Part 1: Prepare the ingredients
  • Part 2: Cook the pasta
  • Part 3: Cook the shrimp
  • Part 4: Combine and serve

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced or minced
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red chile flakes
  • a quarter cup chopped fresh parsley
  • juice of 1 lemon

Instructions

Part 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Step 1

Peel and devein the one pound of shrimp if not already done. Pat them dry with paper towels and place them in a bowl near the stove. Drying the shrimp helps them cook evenly and prevents excess water from splashing into the hot butter.

Step 2

Mince or thinly slice the three garlic cloves and set aside in a small bowl. Chop the quarter cup of fresh parsley and set aside separately.

Recipe Tip: To mince garlic without a press, hold the flat of your knife over the clove and press down firmly with the heel of your hand to crush it first. This loosens the skin so it is easy to peel, and the crushed clove is quicker to chop finely.

TBK Tools:

  1. Garlic Peeler — roll the cloves inside the tube to remove the skin cleanly before crushing or slicing, without needing to pick away the papery skin by hand.
  2. Tool-free: Crush the garlic clove firmly under the flat of a large knife — the skin will split and peel away easily with your fingers.

Step 3

Juice the lemon into a small bowl, removing any seeds with your fingers or a small strainer, and set aside.

Step 4

Measure out the one teaspoon of salt, one teaspoon of black pepper, and one teaspoon of red chile flakes into three separate small dishes or a divided prep tray so they are ready to add without measuring mid-cook.

TBK Tools:

  1. Dry Measure Spoons — tactile spoons make it straightforward to level off each teaspoon accurately by feel without needing to estimate the amount by touch and resistance when levelling off the spoon.
  2. Tool-free: Pre-pinch each spice into a small bowl or ramekin before cooking starts, using a standard teaspoon from your cutlery drawer and levelling it off against the rim of the spice jar.

Part 2: Cook the Pasta

Step 5

Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a full boil over high heat. You will hear a rapid, rolling rumble when it reaches a full boil. Add a generous pinch of salt to the water.

TBK Tools:

  1. Boil Alert Disk — place it in the pot and it will rattle and sound when the water reaches a full boil, so you do not need to stay close to check.
  2. Tool-free: Rest a long-handled spoon across the top of the pot — the vibration and sound of the water will change noticeably from a quiet simmer to a loud, rapid rumble when it reaches a full boil.

Step 6

Add the eight ounces of spaghetti to the boiling water, fanning the strands out as you lower them in so they do not clump together. Stir immediately with a long-handled spoon to prevent sticking.

TBK Tools:

  1. Pasta Portioner — use the appropriate hole to measure out the correct portion of dry spaghetti by feel before adding it to the water.
  2. Tool-free: A bundle of dry spaghetti that fits snugly in a circle formed by your thumb and index finger is roughly two servings; double that bundle for four servings.

Step 7

Cook the spaghetti according to the package directions until al dente — firm with just a slight resistance when you press a strand between your fingers. This is usually 8 to 10 minutes. Stir every couple of minutes to prevent sticking.

Recipe Tip: To test for al dente without a visual check, fish out a single strand of pasta with a fork or spoon and let it cool for a few seconds, then press it between your thumb and index finger. It should bend and give without feeling mushy or completely soft all the way through. There should be a very slight firmness at the center.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Countdown Timer — set it for 8 minutes once the pasta goes in and it will announce the remaining time aloud, freeing you to prep the shrimp pan without losing track.
  2. Tool-free: Use the timer on your phone or any audible kitchen timer set to 8 minutes, then test the pasta by feel and add more time if needed.

Step 8

Before draining, use a ladle or heatproof measuring cup to scoop out about half a cup of the pasta cooking water and set it aside. This starchy water can be used later to loosen the sauce if needed.

Step 9

Drain the spaghetti through a colander in the sink. Give the colander a few shakes to remove excess water, then set it aside. Do not rinse the pasta.

TBK Tools:

  1. Side Strainer — clips to the side of the pot so you can tip and drain the hot water safely with one hand without carrying a heavy pot to the sink or handling a separate colander.
  2. Tool-free: Carry the pot carefully to the sink using both hands and pour slowly into a colander already placed in the sink, keeping your face and hands clear of the steam.

Part 3: Cook the Shrimp

Step 10

While the pasta cooks, place a large skillet or wide pan over medium heat. Add the three tablespoons of butter and let it melt, swirling the pan gently. You will hear a quiet sizzle and the butter will smell nutty and rich as it melts. Do not let it develop a strong, toasted or nutty aroma at this stage.

TBK Tools:

  1. Butter Slicer — press the block of butter through the slicer to cut three even pats quickly and consistently, so you are not guessing at the amount by eye.
  2. Tool-free: Most butter wrappers have tablespoon markings printed on the foil — use your thumbnail to feel the indentations and cut along the third mark with a knife.

Step 11

Add the three cloves of minced garlic to the melted butter. Stir constantly for about 30 to 60 seconds. The garlic will become very fragrant — you will smell a strong, sharp aroma that softens into something sweeter as it cooks. Remove from heat briefly if the garlic begins to smell sharp or bitter, as it can burn quickly.

TBK Tools:

  1. Long Wood Spoon — keeps your hand well away from the hot butter while stirring the garlic continuously, and lets you feel the bottom of the pan to check nothing is sticking.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled spoon to stir — the longer the handle, the more distance between your hand and the hot fat.

Step 12

Add the shrimp to the pan so that each piece lies flat and separate, with no shrimp stacked or overlapping — you should be able to feel space between each one as you place them. You should hear a strong sizzle as they hit the pan. Cook for 3 minutes without stirring, then flip each shrimp using tongs. The cooked side will feel firm to the touch of your tongs rather than soft and yielding.

Recipe Tip: Tongs give you the most control for flipping individual shrimp. Work your way around the pan systematically — for example, starting at the position closest to you and moving clockwise — so you can be confident each shrimp has been turned without needing to check visually.

TBK Tools:

  1. Long Wood Tongs — keep hands well clear of the hot butter and sizzling shrimp while flipping, and give precise grip for turning individual pieces.
  2. Tool-free: Use any long-handled kitchen tongs to flip the shrimp one at a time, working in a consistent pattern around the pan so none are missed.

Step 13

Cook the shrimp for another 2 to 3 minutes on the second side. The shrimp are done when they feel firm and springy when pressed gently with the back of a spoon or tongs — they should not feel soft or squishy. They will also curl into a gentle arc that you can feel with the tongs — the tail and head ends will draw toward each other but still have some give and space between them. Overcooked shrimp curl so tightly that the tail meets the head end with almost no gap, and feel very firm and rubbery when pressed.

Step 14

Add the one teaspoon of salt, one teaspoon of black pepper, and one teaspoon of red chile flakes to the pan and stir to coat the shrimp evenly.

Step 15

Pour the lemon juice into the pan and stir. You will hear a brief sizzle and the citrus aroma will rise immediately. Stir in the quarter cup of chopped parsley.

Part 4: Combine and Serve

Step 16

Add the drained spaghetti directly into the pan with the shrimp. Use tongs or two large spoons to toss everything together, lifting and folding the pasta through the butter and shrimp mixture until the strands feel evenly slicked and no dry or clumped sections remain when you run the tongs through the pasta. If the pasta feels dry or the sauce does not cling smoothly to the strands when tossed, add a splash of the reserved pasta cooking water a little at a time and toss again.

Step 17

To serve, use tongs to portion the pasta onto each of four plates. Grip a generous amount of pasta with the tongs and twist slightly as you lower it onto the plate to form a compact, stable mound that holds together when the tongs are released and does not spread flat. Place the shrimp on top by feel, distributing them evenly across the four plates — if you started with one pound of shrimp, aim for roughly equal handfuls per plate.

Recipe Tip: If you have a kitchen scale, you can weigh the total pasta in the pan and divide by four to get an accurate portion for each plate.

TBK Tools:

  1. Talking Scale — place each plate on the scale and use the audible readout to confirm equal portions by weight without needing to estimate amounts by feel alone.
  2. Tool-free: Divide the pasta into four roughly equal portions in the pan by feel — use the back of a spoon to separate them into sections, pressing gently to gauge their size and resistance before transferring one portion per plate.
