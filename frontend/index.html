Spoken Spoon

AI Recipe Accessibility for Cooks with Vision Loss.

Created by the Intelligent Assistive Machines Lab at the University of Maryland, College Park in collaboration with The Blind Kitchen and Chef Debra Erickson.

About This Tool

Spoken Spoon aims to take standard online recipes and adapt them to be more accessible for blind and visually impaired cooks. The tool focuses on replacing visual cues and formatting conventions with descriptions that communicate the same information through other sensory channels or text alternatives.

This tool is designed based on the guidance of Chef Debra Erickson, founder of The Blind Kitchen. Spoken Spoon is trained off of a dataset of recipes created by Chef Debra, and incorporates her expertise in making recipes more accessible. The tool also integrates suggestions for using assistive cooking tools sold by The Blind Kitchen.

Important disclaimer: Recipes generated by this tool are produced by an AI system and may contain errors or imperfect adaptations. The output should be treated as a helpful starting point and reference, not as a definitive or fully reliable result. We recommend reviewing and testing adapted recipes before relying on them for meal preparation.

How It Works

  1. Paste the URL of any standard online recipe into the form above
  2. The AI reads and parses the recipe content, extracting ingredients and instructions
  3. The recipe is adapted to replace visual cues with non-visual alternatives and clearer descriptions
  4. The Blind Kitchen (TBK) tool suggestions are added where relevant to help with food preparation
  5. The adapted recipe is returned as an accessible HTML file that you can download or view
