Spoken Spoon

AI recipe accessibility for cooks who are blind or have low vision

© IAM Lab at the University of Maryland and Chef Debra Erickson at The Blind Kitchen.

About This Tool

Spoken Spoon aims to take online recipes and adapt them to be more accessible for blind and visually impaired cooks. The tool focuses on replacing visual cues and formatting conventions with descriptions that communicate the same information through other sensory channels or text alternatives.

This tool was co-designed with Chef Debra Erickson, founder of The Blind Kitchen, and the IAM Lab at the University of Maryland. Spoken Spoon is prompted with example recipes created by Chef Erickson and incorporates her expertise in making recipes more accessible. The tool also integrates suggestions for using assistive cooking tools created by The Blind Kitchen, as well as alternatives to these assistive tools.

Important disclaimer: Recipes generated by this tool are produced by an AI system and may contain errors or imperfect adaptations. The output should be treated as a helpful starting point and reference, not as a definitive or fully reliable result. We recommend reviewing adapted recipes before relying on them for meal preparation.

How It Works

  1. Paste the URL of any online recipe into the form above
  2. The AI reads and parses the recipe content, extracting ingredients and instructions
  3. The recipe is adapted to replace visual cues with non-visual alternatives and clearer descriptions
  4. The Blind Kitchen (TBK) tool suggestions are added where relevant to help with food preparation
  5. The adapted recipe is returned as an HTML file that you can download or view
