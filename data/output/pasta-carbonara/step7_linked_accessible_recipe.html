Pasta Carbonara Recipe for Blind and Visually Impaired Cooks

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian dish made with pasta, bacon, eggs, and Parmesan cheese. This recipe serves six people and can be prepared in about 25 minutes, with an estimated total time commitment of about 50 minutes for blind and visually impaired cooks.

Introduction and Overview

This recipe is broken down into 8 steps, each with detailed instructions to help you prepare a delicious Pasta Carbonara. Before starting, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients and tools.

Ingredients

  1. 12 ounces of pasta, any variety (about 3 cups of cooked pasta)
  2. 8 pieces of thick cut bacon, diced small (about 1 cup of diced bacon)
  3. 1 medium onion, diced small (about 1 cup of diced onion)
  4. 2 cloves of garlic, minced (about 2 tablespoons of minced garlic)
  5. 3 whole eggs
  6. 3/4 cup of finely grated Parmesan cheese
  7. 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  8. Salt and plenty of black pepper
  9. 1/2 cup of peas

Instructions

Part 1: Step 1 - Cook Pasta

Step 1

Cook the 12 ounces of pasta according to the package directions. Use a talking timer to keep track of the cooking time.

Recipe Tip: Make sure to reserve a cup or two of the pasta water before draining the pasta, as it will be used later to achieve the right thickness that resists stirring, leaving a trail for the sauce.

TBK Tool: Talking Countdown Timer — announces the remaining time audibly so you don't need to read a display.

Tool-free: Use a traditional timer and have someone assist you with tracking the time.

Part 2: Step 2 - Prepare Bacon

Step 2

While the pasta is cooking, fry the 8 pieces of diced bacon in a pan over medium heat until it is just barely crisp. Remove the bacon from the pan and drain it on paper towels. Pour off all the bacon grease from the pan, but do not clean it. Return the pan to the stove over medium-low heat.

TBK Tool: Long Wood Spoon — allows safe stirring and locating of the pan.

TBK Tool: Straining System — enables safe separation of liquids from solids.

Tool-free: Use a traditional spoon and carefully pour off the grease, or have someone assist you.

Part 3: Step 3 - Sauté Onions and Garlic

Step 3

Add the 1 medium diced onion and 2 cloves of minced garlic to the pan. Cook until they have a sweet and slightly toasted aroma and make a gentle sizzling sound, stirring occasionally.

TBK Tool: Heat Gloves — protect hands from heat when stirring.

Tool-free: Use a traditional oven mitt or have someone assist you with stirring.

Part 4: Step 4 - Prepare Egg Mixture

Step 4

In a bowl, mix together the 3 whole eggs, 3/4 cup of finely grated Parmesan cheese, 1/2 cup of heavy cream, salt, and plenty of black pepper until smooth.

TBK Tool: Wet Measure Cups — tactile cups for measuring liquids safely.

TBK Tool: Dry Measure Spoons — tactile spoons for accurate measurement.

Tool-free: Use traditional measuring cups and spoons, and have someone assist you if needed.

Part 5: Step 5 - Combine Pasta and Egg Mixture

Step 5

When the pasta is done, drain it and place it in a bowl. While the pasta is still really hot, slowly drizzle in the egg mixture, stirring the pasta the whole time. The sauce will become thick and resists stirring, leaving a trail and should have a firm and slightly set texture, with a cooked smell the pasta. Splash in a little hot pasta water if needed for thickness that resists stirring, leaving a trail.

TBK Tool: Talking Thermometer — announces the temperature audibly so you don't need to read a display.

Tool-free: Use a traditional thermometer and have someone assist you with reading the temperature.

Part 6: Step 6 - Add Peas, Bacon, and Sautéed Onion/Garlic

Step 6

Halfway through adding the sauce, add the 1/2 cup of peas, the cooked bacon, and the sautéed onion and garlic. Finish adding the sauce, stirring until it's all combined.

Part 7: Step 7 - Season and Serve

Step 7

Season the pasta with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan cheese.

TBK Tool: Salt and Pepper Cellars — stable, tactile-labeled seasoning containers.

Tool-free: Use traditional salt and pepper shakers and have someone assist you if needed.

Part 8: Step 8 - Plating and Garnish

Step 8

To plate the pasta, use a serving spoon to portion out the desired amount onto a plate. Use a fork to arrange the pasta in a uniform and even texture and tidy manner. Garnish with extra Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of black pepper.

TBK Tool: Serving Spoon — helps with portioning the pasta.

TBK Tool: Fork — assists with arranging the pasta.

Tool-free: Use traditional serving utensils and have someone assist you if needed.

Enjoy your delicious Pasta Carbonara!
