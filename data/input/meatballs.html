Lamb Meatballs with Pomodoro and Ricotta

Sept. 14, 2023, 8:45 AM EDT / Updated Nov. 6, 2025, 6:19 PM EST
Lamb meatballs with pomodoro and ricotta: Get the recipe!
04:21
Geoffrey Zakarian
Cook Time:
1 hr
Prep Time:
25 mins
Servings:
4
Rating:
(16)

Chef notes

Bread and canned tomatoes are truly both unsung heroes of the pantry, and I always keep them on hand for dishes like this one. When I buy a fresh baguette, I usually freeze what I don't use right away so that it's at the ready when I want to make meatballs. In this recipe, the soaked bread and sautéed shallot and garlic mixture act to bind the meat together and help keep the end result moist and almost creamy. Although undetectable in the final dish, these tricks will serve you well.

Meatballs have always been one of the most-ordered items on my menus, and this version with savory lamb and velvety ricotta is no exception. When everyone is gathered around the table, it's the perfect food to pass around and enjoy together to kick off a delicious meal. This is a wonderful recipe to make for your guests at home as well because both the meatball mixture and the tomato sauce can be made a day ahead and stored in the refrigerator overnight.

I will often make a double batch of the sauce and freeze half of it. If you are already going to the trouble, why not stretch your efforts and have a gorgeous sauce waiting in your freezer for the next pasta dish, chicken parmesan or Italian-style shakshuka? The biggest piece of advice I have when making this dish is to season with kosher salt and fresh cracked pepper at every step and taste as you go.

Technique tip: When making meatballs, I like to cook one tablespoon of the mixture in a small skillet to taste for seasoning before forming all the meatballs. That way, you can add more salt or pepper if needed.

Swap option: You can substitute ground beef, ground chicken or ground turkey for the lamb. If using beef, use at least 20% fat, and if using chicken or turkey, use a blend of light and dark meat.
